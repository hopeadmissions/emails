It’s spring break for Hope College! While many students have headed home for the week, others are taking part in an alternative spring break — one that provides purposeful R&R as they bring their Hope experience to other parts of the world. This week, our students are doing things like:

  • Boarding a medical mission boat in Brazil and traveling to villages along the Amazon River;
  • Learning and using sign language with students in Jamaica at the Caribbean Christian Centre for the Deaf;
  • Building a new church for communities in the Dominican Republic;
  • Restoring dignity to those in need through the Mobile Loaves & Fishes Ministry in Austin, Texas; and,
  • Exploring poverty in Denver, Colorado, under the guidance of those who were once labeled as “least, last and lost.”

Also this week, our Baker Scholars are in Tokyo, Japan, learning about international business. The Emmaus Scholars are serving others in Washington, D.C. Fraternity and sorority members are in Houston, helping hurricane recovery efforts. The Wind Ensemble is touring “out East” and Hope spring sports teams are competing “down South.” And, the Outdoor Adventure Club is hiking through the mountains of Arkansas.

Where will you be this time next year? We hope you’ll be with us on a break that makes a difference.
