Want to share the stage or studio to showcase your talents with other dedicated musicians, actors, dancers, and artists? Shape your path in graphic design or creative writing, too? Come to a place where you’ll do all of this while learning from professors committed to your artistic growth and success.

With nationally accredited programs in dance, theatre, music, and visual arts, Hope College has a long-standing tradition of providing outstanding instruction and experience in every major arts area. Our students hone their crafts by participating in Musical Showcase, H2 and StrikeTime dance companies, OPUS magazine, art gallery exhibits, Hope Summer Repertory Theatre, Visiting Writers Series, and much more.

We have amazing facilities for all of this, too. At Hope, we’ve invested more than $50 million in our newest arts facilities like the Kruizenga Art Museum and the Jack H. Miller for the Musical Arts as well as the Knickerbocker Theatre, DeWitt Theatre and DePree Gallery.

Apply for a Distinguished Artist Award (DAA) in:

  • visual arts,
  • creative writing,
  • dance,
  • music, and
  • theatre.

Each DAA grants $3,000 in renewable, annual scholarships to students with strong academic records and outstanding creative abilities.

Whether you plan to major or minor in the arts, there are countless opportunities to flourish at Hope. We know you’ll find your place here to stand out and shine.
