“I’m taking Hope with me wherever I go.” Those are the words of Hope graduate Anysie Ishimwe as she prepares to begin a fellowship with the Global Health Corps.

Anysie will be based in her home country of Rwanda, where she will partner with Gardens for Health International. Focusing on the health of Rwandan families, she’ll build partnerships and develop innovative programs with district hospitals, health centers and the government.

Thanks to her Hope education, Anysie is well prepared to take on the challenges of a changing world. Her classroom studies and off-campus work at Hope — including immersion trips, study abroad in Switzerland and two May terms in Rwanda — equipped her with skills and knowledge to make an impact. What really made an impression on Anysie were her relationships at Hope, especially with her professors. “I didn’t expect them to care so much about me even after graduation,” she says.

Read Hope Alumni’s blog post about Anysie.

Today, Anysie is leading the way with confidence and enthusiasm for bridge-building. “We are building a global community of diverse young leaders changing the face of global health,” she says.

No matter what your vision for the future, Hope College will prepare you to lead, serve and thrive in communities throughout the world.

Sincerely,
Scott Travis
