As a Michigander, you know the Mitten State well. But did you know that Hope’s hometown of Holland, Michigan, was called a “Happiest, Healthiest Place to Live in America”? Are we happy to live in Holland? You bet!

Hope College is a four-season campus, and we couldn’t be happier about that. We love living and learning in a place where each season brings its own Holland-style beauty and fun.

  • Fall: You can still enjoy many warm and sunny days at the beach before the wind gets crisp and the leaves start changing. Plus, pumpkins, apple cider and sweatshirt weather! Need we say more?
  • Winter: Snow means sledding, snowshoeing and skiing. And with Holland’s underground snowmelt system, the streets and sidewalks of downtown are clear for walking and jogging and shopping, too. Some years Lake Michigan will freeze over!
  • Spring: Campus blooms in April. Plus, nearly 5 million tulips planted for the city’s annual Tulip Time and Tulipanes festivals bring downtown Holland to life.
  • Summer: Ah summer! Sunsets along our sandy, picturesque Lake Michigan coastline — just minutes from campus — are spectacular. We savor our summer months swimming, sailing and soaking in Michigan’s freshwater lakes.

See for yourself why Holland is a great place to attend college! Plan your visit to Hope today. We can’t wait to show you around our campus and hometown!

Sincerely,
Bill Vanderbilt
Vice President of Admissions
