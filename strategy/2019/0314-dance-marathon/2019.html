“The path of deciding a major is a stressful and confusing one for many, myself included.” That’s the voice of college senior Addison Huss, a social work major who wrote a recent blog post for the Boerigter Center for Calling and Career at Hope.

Read “Journey to Finding a Major” by Addison Huss ’19 →

Like many students, Addison came to college unsure about where her path would lead. She loved volunteering, coaching and was interested in the public school system. Naturally, she thought she might like to explore a teaching degree, but after her first class, she wasn’t so sure of herself. Fast forward a few years and a lot of exploration, Addison has found her calling in social work and has accepted an internship with a local school district after she graduates in May.

Does the task of selecting a major weigh heavy on you? You’re not alone. Some students come to Hope sure of their calling, but many are not so sure — and many change their minds. And that’s okay! As Addison writes on the blog,

“Although the process of picking a major is daunting, I hope others recognize the goodness found in the journey because it is an opportunity to learn about yourself and the world around you.”

Addison offers five great tips for exploring your options in her blog post. She encourages you to reach out to advisors, professors, friends, family members, and others and to take the time you need. “This is a big decision, but I hope you find peace in knowing that you are not meant to make it on your own,” she says.

Whether you have a major in mind or haven’t considered it too much, we invite you to explore your options with Hope. Get started today by completing your Common Application.
