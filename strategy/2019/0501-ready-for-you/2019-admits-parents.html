As graduation for our seniors draws near, we asked our Student Congress President Jason Gomery ‘19, a Florida native, to reflect on his college-search experience and what drew him all the way to Hope. We think you’ll be interested in hearing what he had to say…

Just a few years ago, I was in your shoes — making a decision about where to go to college. Today I’m preparing to graduate from Hope in May with a degree in business.

To be honest, I never pictured myself at Hope College. I lived my entire life in Florida and always planned to attend a large public university in my home state. At the recommendation of a friend, I visited Hope during my senior year of high school. From the moment I stepped foot on campus, I knew something was different about this place.

After returning from my visit, I couldn’t stop thinking about Hope but I told myself, “I should probably stick close to home.” Still, as May 1 approached, Hope was my top choice. My family encouraged me to give Hope a shot, and I made my deposit! I was excited but still nervous. I had so many questions. You might have the same questions too, so please allow me to offer some insights.

Will I make friends? With so many student groups and activities, it was easy to immerse myself in campus life. I was “on the rope” for The Pull and became a member of Student Congress, which led me to meet some of my best friends.

Where will I spend my breaks? Being more than a 1,000 miles away from home, I knew I wouldn’t be able to get home for shorter fall and winter breaks. No worries. Before the second week of school was over, I had already received many invitations from new friends to join them for fall break. It still amazes me how welcoming the Hope community is!

Could I make it through Michigan winters? Ah, the million dollar question! My roommate, who was from Michigan, gave me great advice about a good winter coat and boots. (Here’s the secret: If you wear the right clothes, the cold and snow are not a problem!) Plus, this Floridian has discovered the beauty of all the seasons. Fall color, winter snow and spring blossoms — they are all beautiful in their own way!

Will Hope prepare me for success after graduation? Yes! I took a business marketing class my junior year that allowed me to gain real-world experience with a real-world client, Steelcase, a global furniture manufacturer. At the end of the semester, Steelcase offered me a summer internship in Grand Rapids, Michigan. Best of all, I’m excited to start my career pursuing my passions after graduation next month.

I hope my story helps you. As you can see, I think Hope is a special place, and I believe you’ll feel that same way about it, too. Submit ${Contacts.Preferred First Name}’s enrollment deposit today.

Sincerely,

Jason Gomory
