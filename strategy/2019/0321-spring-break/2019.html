It’s spring break at Hope College. While many students have gone home for the week, others are taking part in an alternative spring break — one that provides purposeful R&R as they bring their Hope experience to other parts of the world. Some students are also exploring possible career paths with Hope alumni through the Boerigter Center for Calling and Career’s DiscoverWork program.

Some of the things our students are up to this spring break:

  • Serving in Washington, D.C., with faith-based justice organizations and learning about the history of race and poverty in the United States with fellow Emmaus Scholars
  • Job shadowing with Hope alumni at the Center for Disease Control and Prevention in Atlanta, GA
  • Exploring Moab Canyonlands and Arches in Utah with the Outdoor Adventure Club in Utah
  • Exploring career paths and job shadowing at hospitals in Louisville, KY
  • Studying Caribbean marine biology and biophysics in Cozumel, Mexico
  • Performing in Florida and Tennessee with the Hope College Chapel Choir
  • Addressing water quality in Kenya, Africa with Hope’s chapter of Engineers Without Borders

Where will you be this time next year? We hope you’ll be with us on a spring break that makes a difference.
