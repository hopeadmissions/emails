Which college classes should I take? How do I register for classes? What will my professors expect of me? Which major will I choose? When will I sign up for housing?

If these questions sound familiar, don’t worry! You’re not alone. Our campus community is eager to walk alongside all of our first-year students as they transition into college life at Hope starting with Admitted Student Day in April.

Once enrolled, students have the option to participate in a variety special summer programming and peer-led initiatives to help them prepare for a successful transition to college. Some of these programs include New Student Advising Days, the Summer Bridge Program and First-Year Seminar.

“College is one of those things every incoming freshman has not experienced yet so they don’t know what to expect… A lot of times new students don’t know what to ask. With our summer initiatives and other first-year experiences, we want to answer some of their unspoken questions and help to normalize an understanding of expectations.”
— Chris Bohle, Associate Director, Student Life

First year students have so much to look forward to at Hope: New Student Orientation, Time to Serve, The Pull, Nykerk and much more. Until then, we invite you to look ahead at what’s next as you consider becoming a student at Hope.

It’s our privilege to help you make the most of your Hope College experience. We can’t wait to get you started! Submit your Common Application to Hope today.
