We welcomed Hope graduate Julian Hinson ’10 back to campus earlier this week for the annual Martin Luther King, Jr. Civil Rights Lecture.

Julian once made the kind of college decision you’re facing now. The former Hope chemistry and French major is today a fourth-year medical student at Meharry Medical College.

The world opened up for Julian when he chose Hope. He became a Phelps Scholar, participated in student clubs and helped plan Hope’s Critical Issues Symposium. He spent five weeks abroad shadowing endocrinologists in one of France’s premier hospitals. Julian also received the Sigma Xi Research Award in recognition of exceptional undergraduate research, and co-authored two published papers with Professor Dr. Kenneth Brown. He applied — and was accepted — to medical school, and today he serves patients with passion and compassion.

We are proud of Julian and all our alumni, and we are excited about the opportunities that await you at Hope College. Our alumni network is 33,000+ strong, standing ready to support your success this fall and in the years after.
