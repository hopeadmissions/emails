Living on Hope’s campus is as much a part of our students’ education as taking classes, with more than 80% living in college-owned housing. As a residential student, you‘ll experience what “Home Sweet Hope” feels like while learning to be independent and living in community with your neighbors.

First-year students start their college careers living in residence halls on campus. Then, usually junior year, they have the opportunity to move to more independent yet college-supported housing such as cottages, Cook Village townhouses, and even apartments above the shops in downtown Holland. Parking, laundry and high-speed internet are just a few of the perks available when you live on campus.

Read more about residential life in my blog post, “Live Where You Learn.”
