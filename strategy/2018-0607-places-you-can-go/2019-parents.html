Where will your student go to further their education? Paris? Vienna? Shanghai? Mexico City? Or maybe Japan, South Africa, or England? Hope students have access to more than 300 international programs in over 60 countries. We believe all the world is a classroom whether during the academic year or summer.

In fact, half of this year’s graduating class has spent a semester or summer term studying off-campus either domestically or internationally. In their studies abroad, our students’ experiences include cultural, historical, literary, musical and personal enrichment. All are led and taught by Hope professors with expertise in these areas, and all are life-changing in their own way. Here are just a few summer offerings that Hope students engaged in this year:

  • Art, History, and Memory in Contemporary Paris;
  • Vienna’s Musical Tradition;
  • China: Land, Wildlife and Culture;
  • Humor, Communication, and Culture in England;
  • Faith, Race, Community and Intercultural Communication in South Korea;
  • Bringing Hope to the World in Montego Bay, Jamaica;
  • Ecological Theology and Ethics in the Adirondacks Park, N.Y.;
  • Christianity and Race in South Africa; and
  • Irish Culture and Celtic Wisdom.

Off-campus programs are a vital component to Hope’s mission: to educate our students for lives of leadership and service in a global society through academic and co-curricular programs of recognized excellence in the liberal arts and in the context of the historic Christian faith. No matter where your student goes, no matter what your student studies, a world of good can be found in a Hope education. Come visit us and learn more.

Sincerely,
Amy Otis-DeGrau
Senior Director for the Fried Center for Global Engagement
