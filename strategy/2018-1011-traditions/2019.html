Way to go! You’ve submitted your application for admission to Hope College. Students whose application is complete by Nov. 1 will hear back from us with a decision mailed Thanksgiving week. In the meantime, you might be curious to learn more about Hope College traditions and the legacy of being an odd year or even year graduate.

Autumn is prime time for traditions at Hope, starting with the Pull in September. Now in its 121th year, the Pull is an epic tug-of-war contest between freshmen and sophomores held across the Black River. It is one of the nation’s oldest standing college traditions, and one of intense struggle to maintain the even or odd-year legacy.

In October, our freshmen and sophomore classes meet again to battle it out on stage for the Nykerk Cup in another decades-old tradition of competitive song, play and oration. Odd and Even Year teams intensely prepare their performances weeks in advance for one night of intense competition.

Whether you want to join The Pull, Nykerk or something else entirely, there’s a Hope tradition waiting for you in the Class of 2023. As the saying goes, “Odd Year Every Year!”
