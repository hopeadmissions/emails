What can a liberal arts education do for your student if he or she is interested in a career in STEM? Ask anyone on the Hope College Formula Racing team, and the answer will be a lot. And they have the racetrack credentials to prove it.

At the recent international Formula SAE competition in Lincoln, Nebraska, the Hope team comprised of 15 students with various majors outpaced big competition from large universities and technical schools from around the world — finishing 11th out of 80 teams. The best part of that impressive finish: Hope was the only private, liberal arts college in the competition!

Senior Ross Newland, the team’s co-captain and an engineering major, summed up the team’s experience best when he said, “We certainly were the underdogs at the Lincoln event, but that is what drives our team and makes our accomplishment so much more meaningful. Despite a smaller budget and a smaller team, the reason we were so successful is because we have a great team.”

Ross also credits his Hope education with making a difference in his personal story: “My liberal arts education has helped me develop into a well rounded engineer. I cannot stress enough how important that is in today’s industry.”

“The education I received from Hope, supplemented with the applied skills I learned from Formula SAE, have really provided me with an advantage that will help me be successful in both my career and in life.”
