Research, take it for granted.

Students and faculty at Hope College expect to be national leaders in the field of scientific research. This expectation is shared by the Research Corporation for Science Advancement (RCSA) which recently released its list of the top ten liberal arts colleges who have received the most grants in the foundation’s 100-year history. Hope topped the list with 64 grants.

Read about Hope's achievement »

Hope is also first when it comes to the National Science Foundation, receiving more grants for collaborative faculty-student research than any other undergraduate college in the country. One such grant supported Hope’s involvement in the SEED project, an open-source effort to develop and share genomic data. Recently three Hope Computer Science students published the bioinformatics software "CytoSEED" which is currently being used by scientists throughout the world.

Read about this student-built software »

The National Science Foundation recently awarded Hope College Chemistry professor Jeff Johnson a 5-year, $400,000 grant. Johnson's award will support his ongoing research and create a “green chemistry” and sustainability first-year seminar course at Hope.
