I’m Matt DeJongh, one of the Computer Science professors at Hope College. The Admissions Office told me of your interest in Hope College and Computer Science, so I'd like to pass on some information about our program.

Most of what you might want or need to know is available on our website for prospective students.

I'll just highlight a couple of things:

1) we train students in both the practice and the theoretical foundations of computer science, so our majors are equipped for jobs as well as for graduate school. Recent graduates have gone into software engineering, technical consulting, graduate programs in computer science and information science, and law school.

2) all of our classes are taught by faculty in small settings (no more than 20 students to a class).  Most of our classes involve hands-on lab work.

3) our department is a national leader in involving undergraduate students in research. We have ongoing projects in areas such as bioinformatics, electronic textbooks, computer networks, and gaming, with funding from agencies like the National Science Foundation.

4) the liberal arts component of a Hope College education is a great complement to a computer science degree, because employers are interested not only in your technical skills, but also in your problem-solving, communication and teamwork abilities.

Please feel free to email me if you have any specific questions about our program.  If you have not yet visited the Hope College campus, we'd love to have you come meet our students and faculty, and see our computer labs!  Best wishes in your college search,
