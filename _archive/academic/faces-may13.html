Congratulations on your admittance to Hope College! Your decision to come to Hope College and pursue a degree in the Natural and Applied Sciences will be the first step in a successful journey of higher learning.

The Natural and Applied Sciences division extends you an invitation to get involved in a program specifically designed for racially and ethnically underrepresented and/or first generation college students entering their first year. The FACES (Fostering A Community of Excellence in Science) Mentoring Program provides an opportunity for you to be paired with one of our science students as you begin your college career. Your mentor will help guide you through your first semesters of college, and be a resource towards your goals and success here at Hope College.

Components of the program
  • peer mentoring
  • academic skills development programs
  • outreach activities
  • exclusive networking opportunities
  • professional development programs
Benefits of the program
  • FACES participants have a one and two-year retention rate that is higher than students who are eligible but choose not to participate in the program.
  • "FACES provides a wonderful opportunity to foster relationships between freshmen and upper-class students." — 2012 Mentor
  • "FACES connected me with upperclassmen and professors easily, who both gave me great advice." — 2012 Protégé

If you think you could benefit from having a mentor who has walked in your shoes and is ready to walk beside you, a program that is designed to help you make connections to the Natural and Applied Sciences and Hope College, and a program director who is ready to meet with you throughout your college career, then FACES at Hope is the program for you!

This program is free for you!

If you are interested in participating in the FACES at Hope Peer Mentoring Program, please complete the online application by Monday, June 3. Once your FACES application is complete, we will connect you with a mentor. Your mentor and I can serve as information resources as you begin making choices about class selections for the fall semester!
