Let’s be honest: New England is home to many fine colleges & universities, each with the kind of high-quality academic preparation for which you’re searching. However, none are quite like Hope – not only do our professors teach 100% of classes and labs, nearly all of them also collaborate with students on research and creative performance, all in the context of a vibrant, yet voluntary Christian environment.

So, here’s why you should consider Hope:

  • Nationally-recognized programs like Chemistry, Education, Biology, Engineering, Business Management, as well as many unique academic programs for a college of our size: Leadership, Japanese, Neuroscience, Athletic Training, and Nursing, to name a few.
  • Hope was the first liberal arts college to receive national accreditation in all 4 areas of the fine arts: Music, Dance, Art, & Theatre, with opportunities for both majors and non-majors.
  • 89% medical school acceptance rate for first-time applicants over the last 10 years, with similar placement for both dental and law school.
  • The leading recipient among liberal arts colleges of National Science Foundation (NSF) grants for undergraduate research. This means more resources for student-faculty collaborative research and co-authorship of scholarly publications.
  • “U.S. Student Top Producer” of Fulbright Scholars – 3 recipients for 2014-15 and a total of 22 since 2008.
  • One of just 31 colleges & universities nationwide that offers the George F. Baker Scholars Program for business, and the only school in Michigan with this distinction.
