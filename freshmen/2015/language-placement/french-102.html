On behalf of all of my colleagues in Global French Studies, it is my great pleasure to welcome you to Hope College! Preparing for your first semester of college is a very exciting time, and as you begin planning for the fall, I would like to introduce you to some of the opportunities that await you as you pursue your studies in French.

By now you have received your Language Placement Recommendation. Based on your previous experience in French, you have been placed in French 102. To benefit the most from what you have already learned, I would strongly encourage you to take French 102 right away in the fall of your freshman year. As a further incentive, when you take French 102 this fall and complete it successfully, you will earn, in addition to the 4 credits for 102, another 4 credits for 101 at no charge! Not only will this satisfy your general education requirement in foreign language, it will also open many doors to you in your future career endeavors.

Our French 102 program offers…

  • an introduction to basic vocabulary (on clothing, the house, food and the body) and grammatical structures such as the present tense, the passé composé, the command form, the imperfect and object pronouns.
  • a focus throughout the course on French-speaking cultures around the world. We cover five chapters of the textbook Promenades (6-10) which contains short readings and lots of videos on French-speaking cultures.
  • instruction in both French and English to ensure comprehension since students come with different backgrounds (some have had little French spoken in their high school French classes)
  • the option either to complete the language requirement in one semester or to continue with French at the intermediate level.

Additionally, the French Global Studies Program hosts a variety of cultural events throughout the year, including guest speakers, a French film festival, and a weekly conversation group at a local café, and more. It is also never too early to begin thinking about studying abroad on one of our many partner programs in France, Belgium, Switzerland and French speaking Africa.

If you should have any questions regarding your course placement or the French Global Studies program in general, please don’t hesitate to contact me. For more information, including info on our courses, faculty, study abroad opportunities, and especially our cultural programming, I would also invite you to visit and explore our website.

I look forward to welcoming you to campus in August, but in the meantime, have a wonderful summer and à bientôt!
