On behalf of all of my colleagues in Global French Studies, it is my great pleasure to welcome you to Hope College! Preparing for your first semester of college is a very exciting time, and as you begin planning for the fall, I would like to introduce you to all the opportunities that await you if you pursue your studies in French.

By now you have received your Language Placement Recommendation which indicates that you should enroll in Any Beginning Language. In reviewing your application, I see that you have been introduced to different cultures in high school. Now is the time to study a modern language like French to form the basis for a highly marketable skill. To benefit the most from what you already know, I would strongly encourage you to take French 101 right away in the fall of your freshman year. Not only would this help you toward satisfying your general education requirement in foreign language, it would also open many doors to you in your future career endeavors.

Our French 101 program offers…

  • an introduction to basic vocabulary (on school, family, the city and hobbies) and the following grammatical structures: nouns, adjectives, numbers, the present of –er verbs and common verbs such as ‘faire’ (to do) and ‘aller’ (to go).
  • a focus throughout the course on French-speaking cultures around the world. We cover five chapters of the textbook Promenades (1-5) which contains short readings and videos on French-speaking cultures.
  • instruction in both French and English to ensure comprehension throughout the semester.
  • a chance to get ahead! Taking French 101 will enable you to complete the college language requirement with one more semester of French 102.

Additionally, the French Global Studies Program hosts a variety of cultural events throughout the year, including guest speakers, a French film festival, and a weekly conversation group at a local café, and more. It is also never too early to begin thinking about studying abroad on one of our many partner programs in France, Belgium, Switzerland and French speaking Africa.

If you should have any questions regarding your course placement or the French Global Studies program in general, please don’t hesitate to contact me. For more information, including info on our courses, faculty, study abroad opportunities, and especially our cultural programming, I would also invite you to visit and explore our website.

I look forward to welcoming you to campus in August, but in the meantime, have a wonderful summer and à bientôt!
