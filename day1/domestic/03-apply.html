Your remarkable abilities and passion for the field of science have been noticed! I would like to encourage you to sign up for a Day1 program. As a participant in the Day1 research community, you will gain first-hand experience doing scientific research right from the start of your college career. These types of early and significant research experiences have led to Hope being recognized as a national leader by the Council on Undergraduate Research!

In addition to developing great skills that will serve you well no matter what career path you follow, you will build community with other students at Hope that share your interests. There are five Day1 Research communities to choose from. However, the Day1: Phage Discovery program has limited capacity, so if you are interested in this program, sign up soon.

The Day1: Phage Discovery program details include:

  • A two-semester course, guided by PhD faculty members, on cutting edge research in phage genomics (viruses that infect bacteria).
  • Fall semester you will conduct microbiological and molecular research to identify novel viruses.
  • Spring semester you will focus on bioinformatics and comparative genomics to learn about the viruses that you isolate.
  • Day1: Phage Discovery students can choose to live in Lichty Hall, with other Day1 Research Community members.

I encourage you to learn more about this opportunity and the other Day1 programs and then sign up for the programs that interest you. We will begin placing students in Phage Discovery in mid-March and will continue to do so until the program fills, so please sign up for the Day1 programs that interest you today!
