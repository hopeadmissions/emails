The Hope College Day1 Research Communities allow you to do hands-on research that makes a difference in your community from the very start of your first year of college. You can discover a new phage (virus that infect bacteria), tackle an engineering challenge, measure the impact of local efforts to improve the Macatawa Watershed or conduct field geology in northern Michigan. These programs are unique to Hope College and have been recognized nationally as part of what makes the Hope College experience so valuable.

These programs aren’t extracurricular activities, they don’t add to your busy schedule. Instead, they are integrated into your course requirements. Many students report that the experiences help motivate future course and career choices. In addition, the relatioonships they build with peers and faculty prove invaluable. Check out the Day1: Watershed program video to hear more about the value of the Day1 Research Community from students in the program!

Learn more about our variety of Day1 programs today and tell us you’re interested. These courses are open to all first-year students and provide a great stepping stone to other valuable experiences. Unique programs like these are part of the reason our graduates are successful.
