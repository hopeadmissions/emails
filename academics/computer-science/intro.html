I’m Chuck Cusack, one of the Computer Science professors at Hope College. The Admissions Office told me of your interest in Hope College and Computer Science, so I’d like to pass on some information about our program.

  1. We train students in both the practice and the theoretical foundations of computer science, so our majors are equipped for jobs as well as for graduate school. Our alumni are working as software engineers and technical consultants for companies such as Google and Facebook, and are pursuing advanced degrees at schools like the University of Michigan and Notre Dame.
  2. Our classes are taught by faculty in small settings, usually with no more than 24 students to a class. Most of our classes involve hands-on lab work.
  3. Our department is a national leader in involving undergraduate students in collaborative research. We have ongoing projects in areas such as bioinformatics, graph-based algorithms, and smartphone apps for the developmentally disabled, with funding from agencies like the National Science Foundation and the Department of Energy.
  4. The liberal arts component of a Hope College education is a great complement to a computer science degree. Employers are interested not only in your technical skills, but also in your problem-solving, communication, and teamwork abilities.

If you have not yet visited our campus, we’d love to have you come meet our students and faculty, and see our computer labs! Please feel free to email me if you have any specific questions about our program.

Best wishes in your college search,
