Finding community while away at college is an important step to settling in and feeling at home as a student. At Hope, the Phelps Scholars are all about creating community in the classroom and in their residence hall.

Living and learning together provides students in the Phelps Scholars program a “real-life laboratory” for practicing what they learn in the classroom. And, as residents of Scott Hall, they form a strong network of relationships and expand their worldviews by sharing space and thoughtful discussions with students from a variety of backgrounds.

What’s life like as a Phelps Scholar?

Phelps Scholars make a one-year commitment to live in community with others from a broad range of cultural backgrounds. Not only do they live together on campus, they take courses together, travel together, eat meals together, share study space and hang out together in their own lounge in Scott Hall.

Sound interesting? I invite you to learn more about the Phelps Scholars Program.
