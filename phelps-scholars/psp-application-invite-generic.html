Phelps Scholars are a diverse group of first-year Hope students who live out the college's mission of leadership and service in a global society. Through First-Year Seminar, occasional workshops, and optional day trips to Chicago, Dearborn, and other places, they learn about the world and its people while creating a strong community together.

Regardless of your academic interest, being a Phelps Scholar will increase your opportunities for personal and professional success. The ethnic and cultural mix in Scott Hall--including international students from around the world--provides a "real world" experience not otherwise available at Hope.

For more information, visit the Phelps Scholars home page. You may also e-mail me at green@hope.edu or call me at 616.395.7725.

Two Quick Questions (and Answers)

Worried that you'll be isolated on campus? Don't be. Phelps Scholars are more involved in other activities than the average first-year student.

Concerned that it will take too much time? Not a problem. You'll take First-Year Seminar, live in a residence hall, etc. whether you're a Phelps Scholar or not. Spend just one extra hour a week next year to make great friends, enrich your Hope education, and prepare for success in the 21st century.
