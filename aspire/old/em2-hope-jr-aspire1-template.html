As a junior, “college prep” most likely calls to mind the curriculum you’ve chosen in high school that prepares you for college study when you graduate.

Hope advises students to take 4 years of English, 4 years of social studies, 3 years of math and science (ideally chemistry, biology, physics and earth science), and 2 years of a foreign language. Keep taking AP and other challenging courses in your senior year, too.

But college prep also means getting ready for college outside the classroom — preparing for and taking the ACT or SAT, creating a list of colleges to research, making campus visits, and even writing some “practice” college essays.

At Hope, we’re in the midst of “college prep” for you as well: back in the office after traveling to college fairs and high schools to meet you this fall, scheduling visits for you and your family, sending you information, applying for research grants, and building and improving facilities throughout the campus. And each year, a talented and passionate group of Hope students create Hope’s Orientation program to make sure you are warmly welcomed and know the ropes before school begins.
