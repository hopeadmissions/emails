DANCE MARATHON

When the first Dance Marathon took place at Hope in 2000, organizers expected a school of Hope’s size to raise $4,800 for the Helen DeVos Children’s Hospital.

Not surprisingly, students’ hard work and dedication brought in more than $23,000. Each year has involved more students and raised more funds. Last year's event raised over $92,000 and topped the $1 million mark for the total raised in the event's history.

Dancers are on their feet for 24 hours in a gesture of solidarity with the children and families they support. Moralers work 8-hour shifts to coach, entertain and ensure spirits and energy stay high.

A Dream Team of 20 students makes a yearlong commitment to lead more than 600 Hope students involved in the event. Behind-the-scenes, finance and marketing, family relations, catering, special promotions and other committees plan, raise funds and surround the dancers and moralers with everything they need to make the event spectacular.

IMMERSION 2013

Immersion trips give students deeper, richer experiences with what we call the “four corners of justice” — poverty, race and reconciliation, environmentalism and immigration.

Locations change from year to year, but 2013 spring break sites include Pine Ridge Reservation, SD; Little Haiti, Miami, FL; East Palo Alto, CA; and the L’Arche community in Mobile, AL.

Students serve wherever they go, but more, they live in a setting, coming to know the people there with their heads and their hearts. Ministries to children, adults with developmental disabilities, urban youth, or to neighbors in Hope’s home town of Holland allow students to experience issues of justice, and move beyond perceptions that divide one another.
