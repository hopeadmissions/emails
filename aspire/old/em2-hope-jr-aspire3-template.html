If you visit Hope College on a Visit Day, you’ll likely be welcomed by Mayor Kurt Dykstra, who also teaches at the college. Mayor Dykstra’s close ties to Hope reflect the pride and warm welcome that Hope students receive in Holland, an award-winning town.

A few steps from campus, Holland’s restored, historic downtown caters to college students, residents, and visitors year round with a fun mix of quaint, stylish and chill. Find the latest in outdoor gear, ice cream flavors, indy coffee shops, galleries and the newest bestsellers in a three-block stroll. Sidewalk benches and patio tables invite you to linger in warm weather, and streetcorner fireplaces and even heated sidewalks warm shoppers in the winter.

Yearly festivals, such as Tulip Time, Dutch Winterfest, Fiesta, and Tulipanes Latino Art and Film Festival, commemorate the town’s Dutch heritage and today’s multicultural community.

Lake Michigan and Lake Macatawa give the residents and outdoor enthusiasts of Holland year-round enjoyment. Bordered by waterfront, parks and greenspaces, boardwalks and bikepaths, our town will suit your mood for fresh air and great views, many just a stroll from campus.

Bike or walk to the beach from campus year round to swim, explore or gather around a bonfire to watch one-of-a-kind sunsets. Holland State Park, one of Michigan's most popular state parks, fronts on both Lake Michigan and Lake Macatawa, drawing boaters and sailors alike.
