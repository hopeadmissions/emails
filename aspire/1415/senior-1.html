Seasons of Tradition.

Hope abounds with exciting traditions — one recognized by Sports Illustrated is The Pull, the oldest college tradition in America.

Sports Illustrated called this three-hour tug-of-war between freshmen and sophomores on the banks of the Black River an “epic struggle.” As grease-painted pullers heave in response to signals chanted by morale boosters, crowds of students and alumni cheer their encouragement. At the end the winning team takes a celebratory “dip” in the river. This spirited September contest has bonded friends for life, and sparked a spirit of determination to “dig deep” in their college career.

Students who provide moral support during traditions such as The Pull, Nykerk and Dance Marathon are called moralers. This is a hallmark of Hope: students whose designated and critical role is to support fellow students — especially those who are brand-new to Hope.

Five weeks after The Pull, the annual Nykerk Cup Competition involves more than 200 freshman and sophomore women in song, drama and oratory performances. For 80 years this competition has forged close ties among freshman and sophomore women and their sister class mentors as performers, coaches and moralers.
