Picture yourself at Hope College — A vibrant Christian community of over 3,000 scholars from around the world, studying the liberal arts and living on a tree-lined historical campus just minutes from Lake Michigan beaches and steps from charming downtown Holland, Michigan.

What’s it like to live on Hope’s campus?

Most students live in either a residence hall or in a cottage with roommates. Every res hall and cottage is within walking distance to your classes, the library, the Bultman Student Center, dining halls, gyms, coffee shops, Dimnent Chapel and the downtown Holland shopping district. You’ll recognize people nearly everywhere you go. And, best of all, our caring professors have been known to take extra steps to make you feel at home, whether it’s bringing donuts to class or hosting group dinners in their homes.

Okay, so what do students do when they’re not studying?

Hope’s campus life is rich with cultural, scholarly, artistic, faith and service-oriented opportunities. With programs like Campus Ministries, the Center for Diversity and Inclusion, Greek Life, Volunteer Services, intramural sports, The Anchor student newspaper and WTHS student-radio station, and hundreds more — you’ll always have a place to find meaning and friendship.

But don’t just take our word for it; come see campus for yourself! The best way to picture yourself as a Hope student is to visit us. You can schedule an individual visit on most weekdays, or come for one of our special Anchor Days on July 19 and August 2.
