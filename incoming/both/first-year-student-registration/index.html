We look forward to your arrival on campus for the Spring 2017 semester. As a first-year student, we will be working with you individually to create a personalized class schedule that will help you fulfill your general education requirements and intended major requirements.

First-year student registration begins Tuesday, November 29.

Beginning the registration process:

  • Review the general education curriculum.
  • From the College Catalog:
    • Choose the department your intended major/minor falls under (if you have one in mind) and then click the “Majors & Minors” tab to review the program requirements.
    • Click the “Courses” tab to review course descriptions.
  • Explore the Class Schedule to find courses that interest you for the spring semester.
  • Ask lots of questions. We're happy to help!

To register for classes, you will need to contact us. For your first semester at Hope, you are not able to register yourself online. We will register you in-person (by appointment) or via email or phone if coming to campus is not feasible.

Helpful Tips:

  • The First-Year Seminar is not required for first-year students starting in the spring and will be waived for you.
  • A full-time course load is 12-16 credits; most students take 14-16 credits each semester. There is an additional fee for each credit over 16.
  • Find several alternate class options. We may not be able to add you to a closed class.

The Registrar’s Office will work with you for your first registration and you will be assigned to me, Alyssa Boss, as your academic advisor. When you declare your major, you will be assigned to a new advisor in that academic department.

We look forward to working with you!

Alyssa Boss, Assistant Registrar and Advising Systems Coordinator
