Student. Athlete. At Hope, we believe these two identities go hand-in-hand.

Our student-athletes are part of a championship culture that challenges each individual to be their best, physically and intellectually. For many of our students, some of the most rewarding challenges happen abroad.

Read about geology major and soccer player Erin Brophy’s research in Sweden (“Ponies, Swedish farmers, ROCKS. What could be better?”), and history and economics double-major and lacrosse player Joey Williams’ Team USA team handball experience in Croatia (“Reflecting on world-wise experiences because of a world-wide sport”).

We get asked all the time, “Can student-athletes study abroad?” The answer is YES! With off-campus programs in 60+ countries at 300+ sites, Hope offers every student — regardless of athletic commitments — the opportunity to flex their global citizenship muscles. Time and time again, student-athletes return to campus transformed by life-changing experiences abroad and prepared to lead our teams and community in new ways.

We can’t wait to show you around Hope’s campus and discuss the many opportunities that lie ahead for you. Plan your visit today!
