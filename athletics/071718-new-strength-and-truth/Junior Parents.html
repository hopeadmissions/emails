“Be Strong, Be True.”

That’s the Hope College athletics motto, taken from a line in our fight song. We love this saying because it applies to more than sports; it applies to life, too.

All of life.

Every aspect of it.

Such as international study.

Former basketball student-athlete Danny Kosiba learned a whole new meaning for “Be Strong, Be True” when he studied in New Zealand for a semester. His shared insights show a student and an athlete willing to find new strength and truth thousands of miles away from Hope.

"This photo (above) shows me hiking up Roy's Peak while sporting my orange "Be Strong, Be True" Hope t-shirt. This slogan became increasingly pertinent to my time here as I experienced more and more. It takes great strength to throw yourself into the thick of a brand new culture and relearn how to live your life. It's not something you can hide from and you have to be prepared to face any number of new, strange experiences head on. Plus, you have to be true to who you are when you face these challenges; you can't falter. Being true to who I am and where I come from became huge when meeting people from different cultures. My beliefs may be tested and my thoughts may be changed, but no matter what the circumstance, I knew where I came from and what I believed. This Hope shirt served as a firm reminder of that…Not to mention it took a bit of extra strength to trek up a mountainside, too!"

Study abroad is a common part of a student-athlete’s educational experience at Hope. Read about soccer player Erin Brophy’s summer studying in Sweden in 2017.

What will “Be Strong, Be True” do for you? What will “Be Strong, Be True” do for your student? There are so many ways your student can find out here at Hope, both through sports and in life. Come visit us soon. We’d love to tell you more.

Sincerely,
