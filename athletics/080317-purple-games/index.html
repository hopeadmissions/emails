Hope College athletes love to compete, and they love to win. But the toughest battle isn’t always on the field or the court—and that’s when our athletes step up their game.

Every year, Hope College hosts a “Purple Community Game,” an event that raises awareness and funds for cancer and neurodegenerative disease research at Van Andel Institute. For the annual Purple Community Game, Hope trades in its signature blue-and-orange for purple jerseys in a show of support and solidarity of those who battle against these devastating diseases every day.

Read about soccer player Allie Wittenbach and biochem major Philip Versluis, who use their skills to support cancer research →

This year’s game, taking place Sept. 16 at Ray & Sue Smith Stadium, will send the Hope football team onto the field dressed in purple to pursue a victory against Lakeland University — and against cancer.

At Hope College, we’re seeking competitive champions who demonstrate physical and mental toughness. We’re also seeking community champions who have a passion for serving others, leading by example and transforming the world.

Be Strong. Be True.

Sincerely,
